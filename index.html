The real problem

Why capable nonprofits still miss funding

Roughly 85% of grant applications are rejected
Each one takes 30 to 100+ hours to research and write. Most fail before a reviewer ever reads them.

Your team isn't the problem. The process is.

Teams target the wrong funders, miss hidden eligibility rules, or overlook constraints that disqualify them before review begins. Most grant tools optimize for writing speed - even when the opportunity was never viable.

Access problems
70% of private funding is invite-only
• Most funders never post public RFPs
• Public databases overrepresent funders that rarely support small nonprofits
Timing problems
2yr lag in most databases
• Eligibility rules quietly eliminate otherwise strong applications
• Funder priorities shift quietly between cycles
Accuracy problems
33-60% AI error rate
• AI-generated drafts introduce claims that aren't true
• Most tools guess when information is missing

Across all three problems, teams usually discover them too late. By the time anyone flags the issue, the deadline has passed, and many teams never realize there was a better opportunity to pursue.

Sharke surfaces these problems while there's still time to act.

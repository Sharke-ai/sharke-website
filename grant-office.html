Nonprofits with no grant office

You do not have a grant office. You have a list of grants no one has time to run.

When grants are everyone's side job, they become no one's job. The deadlines you meant to hit pass quietly. The funders who would have said yes never hear from you.

The real problem

The problem was never the writing.

It is that no one owns the work from start to finish. Without an owner, every grant starts late, gets researched in a hurry, and goes out on a guess about whether the funder was ever a fit.

That is not a staffing failure. It is what happens to any task that belongs to everyone and no one.

  • Good funders you never found.
  • Deadlines that passed before anyone looked.
  • Hours spent on grants that were never a fit.
  • Drafts started the week they were due.

What Sharke does

Sharke becomes your grant office.

Not one more tool you have to find time to learn. The function itself, run for you, from finding the grant to handing you a draft.

  • Step 1 Finds and watches your funders. Sharke tracks the funders that match your mission, so real opportunities reach you instead of you chasing them.
  • Step 2 Verifies fit before any time goes in. Eligibility, funder history, your size and geography, the timing of the cycle. Each grant comes back with a clear verdict: pursue, wait, or move on.
  • Step 3 Drafts the application for you. Written against the funder's real requirements and grounded in your organization's own materials, not a generic template.
  • Step 4 Tracks every deadline and submission. Nothing slips while you run the rest of the organization.
